Rosalind Franklin

Another forgotten scientist

Rosalind Franklin was an x-ray crystallographer whose work helped uncover the double-helix structure of DNA

  • 1920 - Born in London
  • 1953 - Nature published three papers showing the molecular structure of DNA to be a double helix
  • 1958 - Rosalind Franklin died
  • 1962 - Nobel Prize awarded for uncovering the structure of DNA
